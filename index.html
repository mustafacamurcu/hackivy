IvyHacks

Hackathon: an event in which computer programmers and others involved in software development and hardware development, including graphic designers, interface designers and project managers, collaborate intensively. IvyHacks is a hackathon organized by an enthusiastic pack of students, the Ivy Learning alumni. Over 400 students will form teams and have from the evening of October 14 to the morning of October 16 to build something amazing. The possibilities are limitless; learn a new language, power on your Particle for the first time, try on an Oculus Rift, and turn your dreams into reality. Students will be allowed to innovate using any software or hardware tools, as long as they are working on a fresh project. No previously started projects will be allowed. Over 40 HuskyHacks participants will be chosen based on application, and the event will be held in Matthews Arena from October 14-16.

Location

IvyHack will take place at Istanbul, on Saint Boltoph Street on the east side of Northeastern's Campus. The address is: 16 Saint Boltoph Street Boston, MA 02115

FAQ
What if I don't have a team or an idea?
No problem! Many hackers do not have a fully-formed team or hack concept before the event begins. We will be hosting team-forming activities after hacking begins to help you find the perfect match. If you're a first-time hacker, there will be many others looking for team members with whom you can pair.
Who can attend?
All undergraduate students from any university in the world are eligible. If you are in high school and over 18 years old, you are also welcome to attend.
How much does it cost?
Husky Hacks is free for all accepted hackers. We will provide your WiFi, meals, caffeine, swag, and workspace for the entire weekend.
How big should teams be? Can I hack alone?
We highly encourage you to build with a team. Teamwork is deeply rooted in the hacking ethos, and you'll have a better time building with your brightest peers. Teams should be at most four people.
How do I get to Husky Hacks from the airport?
We recommend taking the MBTA to the venue, as it is the quickest and cheapest way. From Logan, take the blue line train to State street, then transfer to the orange line train towards forest hills and get off at Mass Ave.
What if I have another question?
Please feel free to reach out to us via Facebook, Twitter, or email at huskyhacksnu@gmail.com.
What is a hackathon?
It literally says what a hackathon is in the section right above this one.
What about hardware hacking equipment?
We have partnered with Major League Hacking and will have access to the entire MLH Hardware lab for the duration of Husky Hacks.
What can I build?
Web, mobile, hardware, you name it. Any type of project is welcome. Projects will be judged based on creativity, technical difficulty, polish, and utility by a panel of industry judges.
What should I bring?
All attendees are required to bring their valid student ID for admission. Laptops, sleeping bags, and toiletries are highly recommended.
Is there a code of conduct?
yes.We will be following the Major League Hacking code of conduct.
How will I get to Boston?
It's wicked easy to get to Northeastern from Logan airport, or you can take bus/train from most cities in the northeast and mid Atlantic.
